Eduardo Mutuc

Filipino Metalsmith and Sculptor

Biography

Eduardo Mutuc was born in October 12, 1949 with nine sibligs in Apalit, Pampanga. He is a known metalsmith who dedicated his life in making religious and secular works in wood, silver, and bronze. Mutuc initially worked as a farmer and later turned to woodcarving as a steady livelihood.

However, life did not go as planned, and he earned as little as ₱3.00 a day. A turning point came when he was taught how to do silver plating and Monsignor Fidelis Limcauco commissioned him to create a tabernacle. Through this opportunity, Mutuc discovered his passion for crafting artworks for cathedrals, which would later define his artistic career.

Artistic Contibutions

Eduardo Mutuc is a renowned practitioner of pinukpuk, a traditional technique that involves stamping intricate embellishments onto metal. This method, is used to create decorative metal surfaces that emulate solid metal, commonly seen in images of saints and other religious artworks.

Despite the slow demand for such labor-intensive crafts, Mutuc remained committed to producing his work. He continued to draw inspiration from churches, applying sacred pieces not only to religious pieces but also to his secular artworks, demonstrating his dedication to preserving and evolving this traditional art form.

Visual Works

Silver plating or pinukpuk involves stamping metals for it to have meaning design. Most of Eduardo Mutuc’s crafts are for cathedrals, churches other sacred places such as the cross, tabernacles and calado. Though, he also created secular art, he always make the church an inspiration.
